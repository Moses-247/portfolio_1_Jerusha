About

Dr. Jerusha N Mogaka (she/her), RN, MPH, PhD, is a Postdoctoral scholar at the University of Washington (UW) School of Nursing. Her research focuses on the prevention, testing, and treatment of HIV and STI, particularly among cisgender women in sub-Saharan Africa. Dr. Mogaka is currently the Project Director for the PrIMI Study, a randomized control trial (NCT06203951) that seeks to use an integrated STI testing model to improve perinatal outcomes among pregnant women in Western Kenya.

Her research lies at the intersection of epidemiology and implementation science, with a primary focus on the prevention, testing, and treatment of curable sexually transmitted infections (STIs) among cisgender women in sub-Saharan Africa. I am particularly interested in how innovative approaches, such as mobile health (mHealth) technologies, can enhance service uptake and improve health outcomes in maternal and child health (MCH) settings. My overarching goal is to develop and implement evidence-based interventions that address health disparities and promote health equity in underserved populations.
